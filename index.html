Nyayo Tea Zones Development Corporation

Conserving forests, supporting livelihoods, and sustaining Kenya’s green heritage.

About Us

Founded in 1986 under Gazette Notice No. 265 (State Corporation Act, Cap 466), NTZDC was established to protect Kenya’s gazetted forests by creating buffer zones of tea and assorted tree species, blending environmental conservation with sustainable livelihoods.

Mandate

NTZDC’s mandate centers on conserving gazetted forests via the establishment of buffer belts around forest areas. These include tea plantations and assorted tree species designed to deter illegal encroachment and promote biodiversity.

To date, NTZDC has created over 939 km of buffer zones, comprising approximately 4,064 ha of tea and 5,235 ha of assorted trees, covering nearly 44% of Kenya’s forested landscape.

Operations & Impact

  • Operates across 19 zones nationwide, spanning eight regions.
  • Protects key water towers and forests, including Mt. Kenya, Mau Forest Complex, Aberdare Range, Nandi Forest, Cherangani Hills, Kakamega Forest, and Mt. Elgon.
  • Contributes to improved rural livelihoods through employment, eco-friendly tea production, and infrastructure development in buffer areas.

Tea Processing Factories

Kipchabo Tea Factory (Nandi County)

Established in 2010, NTZDC’s first factory began processing tea internally after years of external processing. It handles ~15 million kg of green leaf annually, generating about Ksh 1.2 billion in revenue. Brands include Chabo Tea, Chabo Tea Bags, Eco Tea, Kipchabo FDS, and the upcoming Najma Tea.

Gatitu Tea Factory (Kirinyaga County)

Commissioned in 2022, this facility is designed with three processing lines. The first processes ~7 million kg of green leaf per year. The factory emphasizes value addition, dedicating ~60% of capacity to specialty and orthodox teas. It supports over 5,000 casual workers and creates ~100 direct jobs while enhancing local infrastructure.
